**The Problem Wealth Copilot Solves**

The world has 280 million cross-border migrants, with remittances exceeding $800 billion annually. Yet financial technology has largely ignored their unique challenges:

- **Currency risk erasure**: A 12% return on Indian investments can become 4% real return after INR depreciation
- **Goal fragmentation**: Education fund in dollars, house deposit in rupees, retirement split between countries
- **Trust deficit**: Robo-advisors feel robotic; human advisors don't understand multi-country complexity
- **Execution friction**: Multiple broker accounts, manual rebalancing, poor timing on remittances

**How Wealth Copilot Works**

Wealth Copilot combines three innovations:

1. **Relationship AI**: Like having a financial advisor who remembers everything about you. "It reminded me of a conversation we had three months ago about my daughter's school," said beta user Priya Menon. "That's when I realized this wasn't just a chatbot."

2. **Cross-Border Intelligence**: The AI understands how currency movements affect your specific goals. It might suggest: "Your house deposit goal is in INR, but you're overexposed to rupee risk. Consider hedging 20% through USD-denominated India ETFs."

3. **Autonomous Execution**: Users set goals and risk tolerance; Wealth Copilot handles the rest — from monthly investments to tax-efficient rebalancing — with full transparency and approval controls.

### Engineering Questions

**Q: Why skills-based architecture vs. fine-tuned model?**

A: Skills give us:
- **Testability**: Each skill has a test suite. We can measure improvement.
- **Debuggability**: When something goes wrong, we know which skill failed.
- **Composability**: Complex workflows = skill chains.
- **Trainability without fine-tuning**: Add test cases, improve prompts — no expensive retraining.

Fine-tuned models are black boxes. Skills are glass boxes.

**Q: How do we prevent AI from giving bad financial advice?**

A: Defense in depth:

1. **Skill guardrails**: Each skill has constraints (max trade size, blocked actions, approval thresholds)
2. **Test coverage**: 90%+ coverage on financial calculations and skill outputs
3. **Backtesting**: All strategies backtested against historical data before production
4. **Human oversight**: High-stakes decisions require approval
5. **Audit trails**: Every decision traced to skill execution for review

**Q: What's the testing moat?**

A: Every conversation, every skill execution, every trade becomes a potential test case. We're building:
- **200+ skill tests** at launch
- **Table-driven tests** for financial calculations
- **Conversation flow tests** for multi-turn interactions
- **Backtesting framework** for strategy validation

This is our moat: the most tested wealth AI in the market.

**Q: How do agents communicate?**

A: LangGraph-based orchestration with structured message passing:

```
User message → Copilot (orchestrator) → Specialist agents (research, trading, planning)
                                      → Skills execution
                                      → Response synthesis
                                      → User response
```

All inter-agent communication is typed and logged.

**Q: What's the latency target?**

A:
- Chat response: < 3 seconds (p95)
- Trade proposal: < 5 seconds
- Trade execution: < 2 seconds after approval
- Portfolio sync: < 10 seconds
